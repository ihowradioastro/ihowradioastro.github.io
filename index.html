General Info

The IAU I-HOW Radio Astronomy Capacity Building Workshop aims to provide a unique platform for students and young researchers from developing nations to learn how to analyze radio data from arrays such as VLA, ALMA, MeerKAT, and LOFAR. It will ideally open a unique opportunity to both increase the science return of the available data archives and encourage the junior to mid-career scientists in the region to learn about Radio Astronomy. A wide range of topics such as star and planet formation, galaxy formation and evolution, the interstellar and intergalactic medium, AGNs, dark matter, dark energy, cosmic magnetism, and transients will be ideally studied through the radio window.

About Lectures and Workshop Activities

The teams of experts include a number of renowned senior lecturers from various fields. One of the objectives of this Workshop is to inspire and prepare young researchers to use future radio observatories like SKA, ngVLA, etc which will revolutionize our understanding about the Cosmos and Universe. These short training and teaching programs are very effective and provide ample opportunities for collaborative research with experts. This particular Workshop also aims to highlight a number of research programs, in line with main research themes, in Iran and to explore new possibilities of Central/West Asia collaborations.

The I-HOW Capacity Building Workshops also promote cultural exchange between participants through various activities outside normal workshop hours. This is the first time the International Astronomical Union (IAU) organizes such a Capacity Building Workshop in Radio Astronomy thanks to the Gordon and Betty Moore Foundation. This IAU/I-HOW workshop is also supported and hosted by the Institute for Research in Fundamental Sciences-IPM.

Registration

There is no registration fee for this workshop. Limited financial support for travel is also available. Accommodation of all the selected participants shall be provided by the organizers. The participants with accompanying person(s) should send us an email on astro@ipm.ir to get accommodation arranged by the organizers. Unfortunately, the expenses for any accompanying person will not be covered by the workshop.

Visa and health formalities

Citizens of most South West, Central Asia countries can obtain an Iran visa at the airport. (Citizens of Armenia, Turkey, and Azerbaijan do not need a visa). More information on visa requirements and application procedures can be found at: evisa.mfa.ir. Acceptance and invitation letters will be sent to those participants who need them for visa applications. If at the time of the workshop there is a new virus outbreak that hinders traveling, the organizers propose to postpone the workshop for a later time. At that moment we will discuss this, and possible other, options such as virtual or hybrid options. The organisers and the representatives of the I-HOW will review the situation every ~3 months to decide the course of action.
